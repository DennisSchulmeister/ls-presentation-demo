Things to Remember

  • The data-chapter attributes marks a slide as a chapter slide
  • All following slides will automatically be indented in the Table of Contents
  • Slides may have a <slide-caption> element with text for the Table of Contents
  • style and class attributes can be used on the <slide-caption>

Structuring a deck with chapters is really easy. Simply add a title slide for each chapter and mark the slide with the data-chapter attribute like this:

<section data-chapter> â€¦ </section>

This will automatically enable chapters in your deck and all subsequent slides will be contents of that chapter. Additionally you may add captions to the slides like this:

<section data-chapter> <slide-caption> Caption for the Table of Contents </slide-caption> â€¦ </section>

Although this example has the caption on a chapter slide, any slide may have a caption. This enables you to build a comprehensive Table of Contents with introductory captions on the chapter titles and index entries on the chapter contents. Take this, for example:

<section data-chapter> <h1>Front Matter</h1> <slide-caption> Welcome learners. Please take your time to read these introductory notes.
